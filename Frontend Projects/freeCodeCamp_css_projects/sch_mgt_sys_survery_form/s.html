Tribute to Professor Wangari Maathai

Professor Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan environmental activist and the founder of the Green Belt Movement. She was the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize for her contribution to sustainable development, democracy, and peace.

Accomplishments

  • 1977 - Founded the Green Belt Movement, planting over 50 million trees to combat deforestation.
  • 1989 - Successfully led protests to stop the construction of a high-rise building in Uhuru Park, Nairobi.
  • 1992 - Played a key role in the pro-democracy movement against President Moiâ€™s regime.
  • 2002 - Elected as a Member of Parliament and appointed Assistant Minister for Environment and Natural Resources.
  • 2003 - Led efforts to rehabilitate the Karura Forest, saving it from land grabbing.
  • 2004 - Became the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize for environmental conservation.
  • 2005 - Launched the Billion Tree Campaign with the United Nations.
  • 2006 - Appointed as a UN Messenger of Peace, focusing on the environment.
  • 2009 - Played a pivotal role in banning plastic bags in Kenya.
  • 2010 - Instrumental in Kenya's new constitution, emphasizing environmental conservation.
  • 2011 - Passed away, leaving a legacy of global environmental activism.
